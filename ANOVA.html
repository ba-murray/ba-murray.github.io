Marketing Campaign A/B Testing¶

A fast-food chain plans to add a new item to its menu. However, they are still undecided between three possible marketing campaigns for promoting the new product. In order to determine which promotion has the greatest effect on sales, the new item is introduced at locations in several randomly selected markets. A different promotion is used at each location, and the weekly sales of the new item are recorded for the first four weeks.

Conclusion¶

The sample violates the assumption that the data is normally distributed therefore Non- Parametric methods were used. The Krusal-Wallis ANOVA results show the P-value is below .05 alpha. Reject the null hyopthesis, there is a significant difference in Sales between one or more of Promotions. The Wilcoxon-Mann-Whitney test results show P-value is below .05 alpha for two of the three comparisons. The most successful promotion based on sales was promotion 1, followed by promotion 3. Promotion 2 was the least successful.
